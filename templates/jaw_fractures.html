Comprehensive Facial Fracture Management

Advanced Reconstruction for Optimal Outcomes

Facial trauma requires immediate, specialized care to restore both function and appearance. Our maxillofacial surgery team has extensive experience treating complex jaw fractures and other facial injuries using the latest surgical techniques.

We understand that facial fractures impact not just physical health, but emotional wellbeing too. Our comprehensive approach ensures precise reconstruction that maintains facial symmetry, preserves function, and minimizes visible scarring.

Types of Facial Fractures We Treat

Jaw Fractures

Mandibular and maxillary fractures treated with advanced fixation techniques including plates, screws, and arch bars for stable healing.

Zygoma (Cheekbone) Fractures

Precise reconstruction of mid-face injuries using minimally invasive approaches to restore facial projection and symmetry.

Nasal Fractures

Functional and aesthetic nasal reconstruction using closed and open reduction techniques to restore breathing and appearance.

Orbital Fractures

Eye socket reconstruction with vision preservation using specialized implants and techniques for complex orbital injuries.

When to Seek Immediate Care

Difficulty Breathing or Swallowing

Any breathing difficulties or inability to swallow requires immediate medical attention.

Severe Facial Pain

Intense, uncontrolled facial pain may indicate serious fracture or nerve involvement.

Visible Deformity

Obvious changes in facial shape, asymmetry, or displaced facial bones.

Double Vision or Eye Problems

Changes in vision, double vision, or difficulty moving eyes may indicate orbital fracture.

Our Advanced Treatment Approach

Emergency Assessment

Immediate evaluation of airway, bleeding control, and stabilization of vital functions for trauma patients.

Advanced Imaging

3D CT scans and digital imaging for precise fracture assessment, surgical planning, and outcome prediction.

Surgical Reconstruction

Open reduction and internal fixation using titanium plates, screws, and specialized implants for stable healing.

Post-Operative Care

Comprehensive rehabilitation including physical therapy, nutritional support, and regular follow-up monitoring.

Recovery Timeline

Week 1-2

Initial healing with soft diet, pain management, and monitoring for complications. Swelling gradually decreases.

Week 3-6

Gradual return to normal diet and activities. Physical therapy begins if needed for jaw mobility.

Week 7-12

Most normal activities resumed. Regular follow-up appointments to monitor bone healing progress.

Month 6+

Complete bone union achieved. Hardware removal may be considered if necessary. Final functional assessment.
