Expert Jaw Pathology Management

Precision Diagnosis for Optimal Treatment Outcomes

Maxillo-facial pathology encompasses a wide range of conditions affecting the jaws, facial bones, and associated soft tissues. From benign cysts to complex tumors, accurate diagnosis is crucial for effective treatment planning.

Our specialized team combines advanced diagnostic imaging with surgical expertise to identify and treat these conditions with minimal impact on function and aesthetics, ensuring comprehensive care for each patient.

Conditions We Treat

Odontogenic Cysts

Radicular cysts, dentigerous cysts, and other cysts arising from tooth development processes that can cause jaw swelling and bone destruction.

Non-Odontogenic Cysts

Nasopalatine duct cysts, nasolabial cysts, and other developmental cysts not related to tooth formation but affecting facial structures.

Benign Tumors

Ameloblastoma, odontogenic keratocyst, and other non-cancerous growths that require surgical removal and reconstruction.

Malignant Tumors

Early detection and comprehensive treatment of jaw cancers with coordinated oncology care and reconstruction.

Warning Signs & Symptoms

Swelling That Doesn't Resolve

Persistent facial or jaw swelling that doesn't improve with time or treatment.

Painless Lumps or Growths

Unexplained masses or growths in the jaw, face, or neck region.

Numbness in Face or Mouth

Persistent numbness or tingling in facial areas without obvious cause.

Loose Teeth Without Cause

Teeth becoming loose without trauma or periodontal disease explanation.

Our Advanced Diagnostic Approach

Clinical Examination

Comprehensive physical examination including palpation, visual inspection, and functional assessment of affected areas.

Advanced Imaging

Digital X-rays, CT scans, MRI, and 3D cone beam imaging for precise anatomical evaluation.

Histopathology

Biopsy procedures and microscopic examination for definitive diagnosis and classification.

Multidisciplinary Review

Expert evaluation by our maxillofacial surgery team with pathology and radiology consultation.

Comprehensive Treatment Options

Surgical Excision

Complete removal of cysts and tumors with preservation of surrounding structures and function where possible.

Reconstruction

Restoration of form and function using bone grafts, advanced techniques, and implant-supported rehabilitation.

Regular Monitoring

Long-term surveillance and follow-up care for early detection of any changes or recurrence.

Adjuvant Therapy

Coordinated care with oncology specialists when additional treatment modalities are required.
