Full Mouth Implant Options

All-on-4 Technique

Strategic placement of 4-6 implants per arch to support a full fixed bridge, providing immediate function with minimal implants.

Individual Implants

Placement of 8-14 individual implants per arch to support separate crowns, offering the most natural and customizable solution.

Hybrid Approach

Combination of implants and bridges optimized for your specific bone structure and functional requirements.

Immediate Loading

Same-day placement of implants and temporary teeth, allowing you to leave with functional teeth immediately.

Ideal Candidates for Full Mouth Implants

Multiple Missing Teeth

Patients missing most or all teeth in one or both arches seeking permanent replacement.

Failed Denture Experience

Those dissatisfied with loose, uncomfortable, or unstable removable dentures.

Sufficient Bone Density

Adequate jawbone volume to support multiple implants (bone grafting available if needed).

Good Overall Health

Healthy patients without uncontrolled medical conditions affecting healing or implant success.

The Full Mouth Implant Process

Initial Consultation

Comprehensive examination, imaging, and discussion of your goals and expectations.

Surgical Placement

Precise placement of 8-14 implants under general anesthesia, typically 2-4 hours per arch.

Temporary Restoration

Immediate placement of temporary teeth for aesthetic and functional improvement during healing.

Final Restoration

Placement of final zirconia or porcelain restorations after complete implant integration.

Recovery and Healing Journey

Week 1-2

Initial healing with temporary restorations. Soft diet and careful oral hygiene to protect surgical sites.

Week 2-4

Gradual improvement in comfort. Progression to softer solid foods as healing progresses.

Month 2-4

Significant healing progress. Most normal activities resumed with temporary restorations.

Month 4-6

Implant integration complete. Final restoration placement and full functional restoration.

Frequently Asked Questions

With proper care and maintenance, full mouth implants can last 25-30 years or even a lifetime. The implants themselves are designed to be permanent, while the crowns may need replacement every 10-15 years due to normal wear.
The surgery is performed under general anesthesia, ensuring you feel no pain during the procedure. Post-operative discomfort is managed with prescribed medications and typically resolves within 1-2 weeks.
We offer bone grafting procedures to build up your jawbone and create a solid foundation. This can be done prior to or during implant placement, depending on your specific needs.
Most patients can return to light work within 1-2 weeks. Full recovery typically takes 4-6 weeks before returning to normal activities and diet.
