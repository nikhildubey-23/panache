The Perfect Blend of Strength and Beauty

Advanced Ceramic Technology for Natural Results

All-ceramic crowns represent the pinnacle of modern restorative dentistry, offering exceptional strength combined with lifelike aesthetics. These metal-free restorations are crafted from advanced ceramic materials that mimic the light-reflecting properties of natural tooth enamel.

Whether you're restoring a single tooth or multiple teeth, our ceramic crowns provide the perfect balance of durability and beauty, ensuring your restored teeth blend seamlessly with your natural smile while withstanding the forces of daily chewing and function.

Types of All-Ceramic Crowns

Zirconia Crowns

Ultra-strong zirconium oxide crowns offering exceptional durability and translucency. Perfect for posterior teeth and patients who grind their teeth.

E.max Crowns

Lithium disilicate glass-ceramic crowns with superior aesthetics and natural translucency. Ideal for front teeth where appearance matters most.

Empress Crowns

Heat-pressed glass-ceramic crowns with excellent light transmission and chameleon effect, blending perfectly with surrounding teeth.

Monolithic Crowns

Single-material construction for enhanced strength and natural appearance. No layering means more consistent color and translucency.

When Are Ceramic Crowns Recommended?

Severely Decayed Teeth

When tooth decay is too extensive for fillings but the tooth structure is salvageable.

Cracked or Fractured Teeth

To protect weakened teeth from further damage and restore function and appearance.

After Root Canal Treatment

To strengthen and protect teeth that have become brittle following root canal therapy.

Large Fillings Replacement

When existing fillings fail or when too much tooth structure is missing for new fillings.

The Crown Placement Process

Consultation & Planning

Comprehensive examination with digital scanning and shade matching for perfect results.

Tooth Preparation

Gentle removal of damaged tooth structure and shaping to create space for the crown.

Digital Impressions

Precision digital scanning or traditional impressions for accurate crown fabrication.

Temporary Crown

Placement of temporary crown to protect the tooth while the final restoration is created.

Final Crown Placement

Precise cementation of the custom ceramic crown and final adjustments for perfect fit.

Benefits of All-Ceramic Crowns

Natural Appearance

Superior translucency and light reflection that perfectly mimics natural tooth enamel, making them virtually indistinguishable.

Biocompatibility

Metal-free materials eliminate allergic reactions and gum tissue discoloration, ensuring optimal oral health.

Exceptional Strength

Modern ceramics withstand chewing forces and resist fracture, providing long-lasting durability.

Stain Resistance

Non-porous surface resists staining from coffee, tea, wine, and other common staining agents.
